PLANTWALLK · COST ACTION CA24116

Advancing Plant Cell Wall Knowledge.

PlantWallK brings together researchers from imaging, chemistry, biophysics, computation and modelling to overcome fragmented knowledge and methods.

Learn more Members How to join

About PlantWallK

Plant cell walls are essential for plant development and survival, providing plants with numerous key functions. Beyond their biological role, cell walls represent a major carbon sink and a key renewable resource for food, feed, materials, and bioenergy.

Although our understanding of this complex and dynamic system has greatly advanced, important knowledge gaps remain, which limits the effective use of plant cell walls.

The Action brings together researchers from imaging, chemistry, biophysics, computation and modelling to overcome fragmented knowledge and methods. Multidisciplinary cooperation of PlantWallK aims to improve how we visualise, analyse and simulate cell walls, build shared data and knowledge infrastructure, and create a common language for this community.

WG1

Cell wall analysis methods and probes

Advancing methodologies for analyzing the mechanical and biochemical properties of plant cell walls.

WG1 details

The working group focuses on advancing methodologies for analyzing the mechanical and biochemical properties of plant cell walls. It promotes the development, evaluation, and integration of innovative techniques through interdisciplinary collaboration, including conferences, workshops, training activities, and short-term scientific missions. As a central pillar of the Action, its progress underpins the effectiveness and impact of all other working groups.

Main tasks

  1. Create a comprehensive, standardized inventory of existing methods for mechanical and biochemical cell wall analysis, including imaging, analytical techniques, and molecular recognition tools, with emphasis on quantitative, reproducible approaches applicable to plant and crop materials.
  2. Identify methodological gaps and introduce novel approaches, particularly in situ and in vivo measurement techniques, by transferring suitable methods from other research fields such as biomedical and material sciences.
  3. Establish a sustainable framework for developing and validating new tools, including imaging strategies and molecular probes, supported by coordinated STSMs, joint grant applications, open data repositories, and hands-on training to enable long-term adoption by the scientific community.
  4. Monitor, document, and disseminate progress in method development by compiling validated and emerging techniques into an openly accessible, continuously updated database hosted on the Action website, thereby supporting future experimental practice and broad community uptake.

WG2

Advanced computing tools in image and data analysis

Advanced image and data analysis for cell wall research, with strong emphasis on deep learning.

WG2 details

The working group focuses on the development and dissemination of advanced image and data analysis methodologies for plant cell wall research, with a strong emphasis on deep learning–based approaches. It promotes the adoption of these methods through interdisciplinary collaboration, inventories of existing tools, workshops, and coordinated engagement with analysis specialists and research infrastructures. In parallel, it aims to build long-term expertise by training and integrating early-career researchers in modern computational analysis strategies.

Main tasks

  1. Map and consolidate existing image and data analysis approaches used in plant cell wall research by organizing an interdisciplinary conference involving tool developers, deep learning specialists, research infrastructures, and industry stakeholders.
  2. Identify analytical gaps and implement novel computational strategies by coordinating collaborations with mathematicians, data scientists, and image analysis experts, and translating these strategies into targeted, community-oriented workshops.

WG3

Interdisciplinary approach for multiscale modeling

Advancing multiscale, data-driven modelling through interdisciplinary collaboration.

WG3 details

Working Group 3 focuses on advancing multiscale, data-driven modeling in plant cell wall research through strong interdisciplinary collaboration. It brings together expertise from biology, biochemistry, physics, mathematics, and computational sciences to address challenges related to complex multimodal datasets. Through coordinated discussions, conferences, training activities, and joint grant development, WG3 aims to establish a sustainable interdisciplinary community with long-term impact beyond the Action.

Main tasks

  1. Facilitate effective dialogue between experimental and modeling communities by presenting complex multiscale imaging data to mathematicians and computer scientists and jointly identifying modeling solutions relevant to plant cell wall research.
  2. Strengthen integration between biologists and modeling experts through a structured STSM program that promotes knowledge exchange and the development of new multiscale modeling modules.
  3. Address infrastructural challenges related to big data handling, including computational capacity and large-scale data storage, by defining strategic solutions in collaboration with European research infrastructures such as EOSC and Euro-Bioimaging.
  4. Build long-term modeling competence through education and training, by developing an interactive interdisciplinary course for undergraduate and graduate students focused on multiscale data evaluation and big data analysis in cell wall research.
